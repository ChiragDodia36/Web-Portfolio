3 Approaches for Your Scroll-Driven 3D Portfolio

Each approach uses Next.js + Three.js (React Three Fiber) + GSAP ScrollTrigger. The difference is in the 3D storytelling concept.

A

The Orbit — Recommended

Your career as a journey through space. The camera orbits around a central floating geometry (a polyhedron that morphs per section). Each scroll section transitions the camera to a new "planet" or station.

The Scroll Journey:
  1. Hero: Camera drifts through a starfield, your name assembles from floating particles
  2. About: Camera pulls back to reveal a glowing orb — your "core" — with orbiting rings of tech icons
  3. Skills: Tech stack icons orbit in 3D constellation groups (Mobile, Backend, AI/ML, DevOps)
  4. Projects: Each project is a floating "card station" the camera flies to — hover for details
  5. Experience: A 3D timeline ribbon stretching through space — DotMinds → L&T → IU
  6. Contact: Camera settles on a quiet platform with a glowing contact form

Pros

  • Most immersive and cinematic
  • Unique spatial memory — visitors remember the "space journey"
  • Natural scroll pacing

Cons

  • Heaviest 3D — needs performance optimization
  • Most complex to build
B

The Layers — Clean & Architectural

Flat content planes stacked in 3D depth. As you scroll, each section slides/rotates into view like architectural layers peeling apart. Think: Apple product reveal meets developer portfolio.

The Scroll Journey:
  1. Hero: Your name on a floating glass plane that tilts with mouse movement
  2. About: Panel slides in from depth, text reveals line by line with parallax
  3. Skills: Tech icons on floating glass cards that fan out in 3D space
  4. Projects: Project cards arranged in z-depth, scroll to bring each forward
  5. Experience: Timeline cards stacked in perspective, unstacking as you scroll
  6. Contact: Final plane glides forward, form with subtle glass morphism

Pros

  • Lighter on GPU — more accessible
  • Easier to maintain content
  • Feels premium without being overwhelming

Cons

  • Less "wow" factor than a full 3D world
  • More common pattern (though still rare when done well)
C

The Machine — Code-as-Art

Your portfolio as a living machine/circuit board. Scrolling powers the machine — particles flow through connections, gears turn, data visualizations pulse. Your skills and projects are nodes in this machine.

The Scroll Journey:
  1. Hero: A dormant machine awakens — particles begin flowing, your name illuminates
  2. About: Camera follows a particle stream to your story, text embedded in the machine
  3. Skills: Skill nodes light up in a neural-network-like 3D graph
  4. Projects: Each project is a "module" in the machine — click to expand
  5. Experience: Data flows through a pipeline representing your career progression
  6. Contact: The machine's "output terminal" — a sleek form

Pros

  • Most thematically connected to engineering
  • Particle systems are visually stunning
  • Unique metaphor nobody else has

Cons

  • Can feel cold if not balanced with personal elements
  • Complex particle system management
My Recommendation: Approach A — The Orbit

It delivers the strongest "first impression" factor. The space metaphor scales naturally — adding new projects/skills just means adding new orbital stations. Combined with your clean & premium tone, it'll feel like a luxury space interface rather than a gimmick. The performance concerns are manageable with proper LOD, lazy loading, and progressive enhancement for mobile.
